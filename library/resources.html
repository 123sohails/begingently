Why We Link Instead of Host

To ensure authenticity and ease, this platform gently guides you to well-established Islamic resources rather than hosting large collections ourselves. Our role is to help you know where to begin — safely and calmly.

💻 Trusted Islamic Websites

When you want quick, reliable answers or classical texts, these sites are widely used by scholars and students alike. Always verify advice by cross-referencing sources.

  • Sunnah.com – searchable collection of Hadith with translations.
  • IslamQA – legal opinions (fatwas) based on Quran and Hadith.
  • Quran.com – translations, recitations, and tafsir.

📚 Recommended Books for Reverts

Books can provide depth and context. Start with simple, welcoming titles written for new Muslims.

  • A Brief Guide for New Muslims by Muhammad Rassoul.
  • Islam for Beginners by Osho (for general spiritual perspective).
  • The Ideals of Islam by Imam al-Ghazali.

🎥 Video & Audio Channels

Sometimes listening or watching makes learning easier. These scholars and teachers have YouTube channels and podcasts aimed at English speakers.

  • Bayyinah Institute (Nouman Ali Khan)
  • Yasir Qadhi
  • Mufti Menk
  • QuranicAudio.com – recitations by dozens of qaris.

🤝 Online Communities & Support

Connecting with other Muslims—especially fellow reverts—can provide encouragement and answers to everyday questions.

  • Islam Stack Exchange – Q&A forum moderated by scholars.
  • Reddit r/Islam – active community discussions.
  • New Muslim Support Groups (Facebook).
